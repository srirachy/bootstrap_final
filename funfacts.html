Hummingbirds are called such because of the humming noise their wings create since they move so quickly.

Think you're the only one that could move backwards? Well hummingbirds could fly backwards. They are also the only birds that could do so.

Hummingbirds are the smallest migrating bird. They typically travel alone for up to 500 miles at a time. Something to think about on your next solo road trip.

Imagine communicating by screaming. That's exactly how swifts communicate and at a very high pitch in comparison to their size.

Common swifts could fly up to 68 miles per hour, while their larger variants could fly up to 105 miles per hour.

As cute as swifts are, they are not ideal pets. By nature, they mirgrate to different destinations during winter time. They're nice to watch while they are migrating though.

Ever wonder the divorce rate for paired up seagulls? 0% because they don't have weddings. But more importantly, they pair up for life.

Instead of screaming, seagulls communicate with a range of body movements and vocalizations.

Seagulls became the state bird of Utah because they dealt with a plague of crickets for Mormon settlers.

Penguins are known as the water birds. They use a technique called porpoising to move quickly through water.

When penguins need to move quickly over ice, they use a technique called toboganning which is when they do the penguin slide.

Ever wonder if you could see your house from space? If not, then the next best thing is that you can see penguin poo from space.

Instead of screaming or complex body movements, swallows communicate through different songs.

The term for baby swallows is nestlings.

Swallows are a gentle species who are more interested in flying and adapt well to human habitation.
